Before getting your vaccine

When to consult your doctor

For some people, the decision to get vaccinated will require consideration of risks versus benefits.

You should consult your doctor or a health professional if you:

  • are immunocompromised due to a disease or treatment, such as chemotherapy
  • have an autoimmune condition
  • are pregnant or breastfeeding

What vaccine will you get

There are currently no vaccine safety issues reported following COVID-19 vaccination.

Each vaccine developed for COVID-19 may have different ingredients.

Some vaccines require 2 doses to activate the body’s immune system, so that it can recognize and respond to future infections of COVID-19. Your health care provider will choose the vaccine that’s most appropriate for your individual situation, based on:

  • age
  • underlying health conditions
  • other factors

Common side effects and reactions

After getting vaccinated, it's common to have temporary side effects. These can last a few hours to a few weeks after vaccination.

Common vaccine side effects may include:

  • mild fever
  • flu-like symptoms, such as:
    • chills
    • fatigue
    • joint pain
    • headache
    • muscle aches
  • redness, soreness or swelling in the arm or leg where the vaccine was given

You can take medicine to help with any pain or to lower a fever. Ask your health care provider what they recommend.

Other rare reactions that could occur after a vaccine include:

  • a fever above 40°C (104°F)
  • swelling and an itchy rash where the vaccine was given
  • an allergic (anaphylactic) reaction

If you have an allergic reaction to a COVID-19 vaccine

If you witness or experience a reaction to a COVID-19 vaccine, report it to your health care provider. They're required to report it to their local public health authority. The public health authority then reports them to the Public Health Agency of Canada.

If there is a safety issue with COVID-19 vaccines, Health Canada will take appropriate action.

Health Canada and the Public Health Agency of Canada share the responsibility for ongoing monitoring. Learn more about how Canada makes sure vaccines are safe:

After getting vaccinated

Until a large number of people across Canada are vaccinated, public health measures are still needed to protect everyone and minimize the spread of COVID-19.

At the moment, getting vaccinated doesn’t affect travel restrictions and quarantine requirements.
