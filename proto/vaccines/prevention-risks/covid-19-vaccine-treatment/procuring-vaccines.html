COVID-19 vaccine delivery and supplies

Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) is working with the Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC), Health Canada and Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada to identify, purchase and deliver vaccine candidates and supplies.

COVID-19 vaccine agreements and deliveries

On behalf of the PHAC, and based on advice from the COVID-19 Vaccine Task Force, PSPC is leading negotiations and finalizing agreements with suppliers of COVID-19 vaccine candidates.

To date, the Government of Canada has invested over $1 billion to secure access to promising vaccine candidates.

This includes up-front payments that companies require to support vaccine development, testing, and at-risk manufacturing. Subsequent payments are contingent on vaccines passing clinical trials and obtaining regulatory approval.

These investments guarantee Canada’s earliest possible access to the production of an approved COVID-19 vaccine.

All vaccine candidates will require Health Canada authorization prior to being used to vaccinate Canadians.

COVID-19 Vaccine Global Access Facility

The Government of Canada is also participating in the COVID-19 Vaccine Global Access (COVAX) Facility, a global procurement mechanism that will help develop and deliver safe, equitable and accessible COVID-19 vaccines. Participation in the COVAX facility also gives Canada access to 6 additional vaccine candidates.

The Government of Canada is committing approximately $220 million to the facility to procure up to 15 million vaccine doses for Canadians. An additional $220 million will be channeled through the COVAX Advance Market Commitment to purchase doses for low and middle-income countries, increasing their access to a safe and effective COVID-19 vaccine.

Vaccine supplies

Supplies for vaccinating Canadians

The Government of Canada is purchasing the necessary supplies for the administration of different vaccines. Contracts are in place for personal protective equipment and ancillary supplies such as:

  • needles
  • syringes
  • swabs
  • gauze

Contracting information for these and other COVID-19 supplies

Freezers and dry ice

Given the complexity of the eventual distribution of vaccines, such as the need to transport and store vaccines at specific temperatures, PSPC is also procuring other goods and services required to support the eventual distribution of a vaccine to Canadians.

  • PSPC, on behalf of PHAC, awarded a contract to Fisher Scientific, valued at $1,144,032.33, and to ESBE Scientific, valued at $868,944.07, for the purchase of 26 freezers at -80 degrees Celsius and 100 freezers at -20 degrees Celsius
  • A Request for Standing Offers was issued for supply and delivery of dry ice across the country
  • Requests for Proposals for additional freezers were issued on November 27
    • Ultra-low temperature freezers
    • Ultra-low temperature under counter and portable freezers

Logistics and tracking

End-to-end logistics

PSPC is contracting with one or more logistics service providers that will support PHAC in delivering vaccines to provincial and territorial authorities or points of administration.

Following the Invitation to Qualify process for an end-to-end logistics solution for COVID-19 vaccines, a Request for Proposals was issued directly to four prequalified bidders. They are:

  • Federal Express Canada Corp. and Innomar Strategies Inc.
  • Kuehn + Nagel Ltd.
  • McKesson Canada Corporation
  • UPS Healthcare
