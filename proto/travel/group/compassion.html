Providing (non-professional) care for a family member

In some cases, foreign nationals may enter Canada to provide care to family members, even if they aren't considered immediate family.

Coming from the U.S. (directly or indirectly) as a foreign national

You may be allowed to enter Canada to provide care for a family member if you have compelling evidence to prove your entry is necessary.

What you need to have with you when you request entry into Canada:

  • a written letter from a doctor proving your family member requires care
  • reasons why no other care option is possible
  • details of the type of care you will be providing
  • any other details that would strengthen your case that this care is necessary and that you must be the one to provide it

You must comply with:

  • mandatory quarantine requirements
  • provincial or territorial restrictions

We have strict travel measures in place to control the spread of COVID-19. Because of this, no one can guarantee you will be allowed to enter Canada. A government representative makes the final decision at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time.

Coming from all other countries as a foreign national

You will have to request a family reunification certificate. To apply for one, you must be able to show that your family member needs care, and why you are the only one able to provide that care.

You must comply with:

  • mandatory quarantine requirements
  • provincial or territorial restrictions
Request written authorization for family reunification from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)

To request a family reunification certificate, email us at IRCC.COVID-TravelExemptions-Exemptionsdevoyage-COVID.IRCC@cic.gc.ca

When you contact us, you must provide:

  • your full name
  • your date of birth
  • your unique client identifier (UCI)
  • your passport number
  • a written letter from a doctor proving your family member requires care
  • reasons why no other care option is possible
  • details of the type of care you will be providing
  • any other details that would strengthen your case that this care is necessary and that you must be the one to provide it
  • proof of relationship with your family member in Canada
  • proof of your family member's citizenship, or permanent or temporary resident status
  • proof of visa or eTA

We’ll contact you within 5 business days of getting your email and tell you what to do next.

We have strict travel measures in place to control the spread of COVID-19. Because of this, no one can guarantee you will be allowed to enter Canada. A government representative makes the final decision at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time.

Other types of care for family members

At this time, Canada is not permitting entry to foreign nationals for any other care providing reasons.

Foreign nationals cannot enter Canada to provide:

  • childcare for your relatives
  • support when your family member is already in a healthcare facility or has other care support

You may still be able to enter Canada to reunite with immediate family.

Foreign nationals reuniting with immediate family

Canadians returning to or travelling within Canada

If you are a Canadian citizen, permanent resident of Canada, person registered under the Indian Act, or a protected person, you are allowed to enter Canada. Some provinces and territories have entry restrictions in place that could limit your ability to travel to provide care, childcare and other support.

You must comply with:

  • mandatory quarantine requirements if returning to Canada
  • provincial or territorial restrictions if travelling within Canada

Funerals, sitting vigil and family support

We recognize that this is a very difficult time for families who are facing grief and loss of loved ones.

However, for foreign nationals, attending a funeral or sitting vigil alone isn't enough reason to enter Canada at this time.

Coming from the U.S. (directly or indirectly) as a foreign national

You must provide compelling reasons why your entry is necessary at the time of entry into Canada for consideration. You must also explain how you plan to meet the 14-day mandatory quarantine requirements.

Examples of reasons why your entry may be allowed

  • Executor of the deceased's estate or will
  • Providing emotional support or care for remaining family in Canada that can only be done in person

Examples of meeting quarantine

  • You can complete the mandatory quarantine period before the funeral
  • You are staying with family or friends as long as you will not expose individuals that are not part of your travel group, and you will not be in contact with a person at risk of more severe outcomes from COVID-19

You must comply with:

  • mandatory quarantine requirements
  • provincial or territorial restrictions

We have strict travel measures in place to control the spread of COVID-19. Because of this, no one can guarantee you will be allowed to enter Canada. A government representative makes the final decision at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time.

Coming from all other countries as a foreign national

You will have to request a family reunification certificate to be considered. To apply for one, you must be able to show that your presence in Canada is necessary to handle any family affairs. You will also need to explain how you plan to meet the 14-day mandatory quarantine restrictions.

You must comply with:

  • mandatory quarantine requirements
  • provincial or territorial restrictions
Request written authorization for family reunification from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)

To request an authorization, email IRCC.COVID-TravelExemptions-Exemptionsdevoyage-COVID.IRCC@cic.gc.ca

You must provide your:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • unique client identifier (UCI)
  • passport number
  • detailed reason for travel, such as sorting out an estate or will, providing emotional support for remaining family, etc.
  • proof of relationship with an immediate family member in Canada
  • proof of your family member's citizenship, or permanent or temporary resident status
  • proof of visa or eTA
  • reasons that would strengthen your case that your presence is necessary

We’ll contact you within 5 business days of getting your email and tell you what to do next.

We have strict travel measures in place to control the spread of COVID-19. Because of this, no one can guarantee you will be allowed to enter Canada. A government representative makes the final decision at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time.

Canadians returning to or travelling within Canada

If you are a Canadian citizen, permanent resident of Canada, person registered under the Indian Act, or a protected person, you are allowed to enter Canada. Some provinces and territories have entry restrictions in place that could limit your ability to travel for funerals, sitting vigil, and compassionate reasons.

You must comply with:

  • mandatory quarantine requirements if returning to Canada
  • provincial or territorial restrictions if travelling within Canada
