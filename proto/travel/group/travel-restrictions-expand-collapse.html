Who can come to Canada

The Government of Canada has put in place an emergency order under the Quarantine Act. It applies to all travellers arriving in Canada. Its purpose is to slow the spread of COVID-19 in Canada. Failure to comply with this order is an offence under the Quarantine Act.

Canadian citizens, permanent residents, persons registered under the Indian Act, or protected persons
You have symptoms of COVID-19
Returning to Canada by air

You will not be allowed in Canada by air if you have symptoms consitent with COVID-19.

Returning to Canada by land or sea

You can enter Canada by land or by sea. You will not be allowed by air.

If you have symptoms, you must follow a mandatory isolation period of 14 days.

For information on compliance, go to Complying with the emergency order

You do not have symptoms of COVID-19

You may be able to enter Canada.

You must follow a mandatory quarantine period of 14 days.

For information on compliance, go to Complying with the emergency order

Foreign nationals (including from the United States)
You have symptoms of COVID-19

You will not be allowed in Canada if you are a foreign national and you have symptoms consitent with COVID-19.

You do not have symptoms of COVID-19

Why are you travelling to Canada:

For leisure reasons

You will not be allowed in Canada by air if you are a foreign national and you are travelling to Canada for leisure reasons.

To visit your immediate family

You may be allowed in Canada to visit an immediate family member.

An immediate family is defined as a :

    • spouse or common-law partner
    • dependent child
    • dependent child of a dependent child
    • parent or step-parent
    • guardian or tutor

    For more information, go to Who is considered immediate family.

You must follow a mandatory quarantine period of 14 days.

For information on compliance, go to Complying with the emergency order.

To study

If you’re an international student who has a valid study permit, or were approved for a study permit on or before March 18, 2020, you may be able to come to Canada.

If you’re travelling by air, you need to pass a health check conducted by an airline before you’ll be allowed to board your flight. Anyone who shows symptoms of COVID-19 will not be allowed to enter Canada by air.

You must follow a mandatory quarantine period of 14 days.

For information on compliance, go to Complying with the emergency order.
