Who can come to Canada - Foreign national without symptoms - Essential service

You indicated that:

  • you are a foreign national
  • you are not showing symptoms consistent with COVID-19
  • you are not the immediate family member of a Canadian citizen or a landed permanent resident
  • you are a person who does not pose a significant harm to public health, in the opinion of the Chief Public Health Officer of Canada, and who will provide an essential service while in Canada

Based on your answers, you may be allowed in Canada. The final decision is taken at the border, by a border officer.

  • Generally, you must follow a mandatory quarantine period of 14 days
  • You may be exempt from quarantine if you are performing certain essential work
  • You may have to follow certain provincial or territorial requirements, depending on your destination.
Quarantine exempt essential work

To be considered for exemption, you must be entering Canada to work as:

  • a person or any person in a class of persons whom the Chief Public Health Officer determines will provide an essential service
  • a provider of emergency services, including medical services, for the protection or preservation of life or property
  • a licensed health care professional with proof of employment in Canada, as long as you do not directly care for persons 65 years of age or older within the 14-day period that begins on the day you enter Canada
  • a person, including a captain, deckhand, observer, inspector, scientist and any other person supporting commercial or research fishing-related activities, who enters Canada aboard a Canadian fishing vessel or a foreign fishing vessel as defined in subsection 2(1) of the Coastal Fisheries Protection Act, for the purpose of carrying out fishing or fishing-related activities, including offloading of fish, repairs, provisioning the vessel and exchange of crew
