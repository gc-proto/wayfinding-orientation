Based on the information you provided, your place of quarantine is likely not suitable. You may not be allowed to quarantine at this location. Please make alternative arrangements within your means, including a hotel or other suitable location.

Your place of quarantine is not suitable because:

You cannot quarantine in group living environments. This means a location where a number of people reside in close proximity for either a limited or an extended period of time. In most cases, residents share common rooms such as a kitchen or bathroom and they may or may not have private sleeping quarters.

The risk of acquiring or transmitting COVID-19 infections may be elevated in group living environments.

Some examples include:

  • industrial camps
  • group residences or homes
  • hostels
  • construction trailers
  • student residences (unless you’ve received prior authorization)
  • residential care or long-term care facilities
  • sharing a small apartment
  • living in the same household with large families or many people
  • having housemates who haven’t travelled with you that you can’t avoid

If a government representative at the port of entry determines that your quarantine plan is not suitable, you will be required to stay in a PHAC approved facility for the 14-day mandatory quarantine period at your expense. [wording to be confirmed with Cabinet approval of plan]

If you don’t submit your plan through ArriveCAN prior to your return to Canada, you may be subject to fines when you arrive in Canada.
