Assistance with COVID-19 travel restrictions and requirements

We have strict travel measures in place to control the spread of COVID-19. Because of this, no one can guarantee you will be allowed to enter Canada.

Final determination regarding entry is made by a government representative at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time of entry into Canada.

Answers to top questions

If you think you may be exempt from mandatory requirements

You may be exempt from the requirement to quarantine if you are entering Canada for the purpose of performing cross-border work, essential services, medical care or trade or transport.

At this time, proof of having a vaccine does not exempt you from testing or quarantine.

If you think you may be exempt from mandatory requirements, check the details for:

  • Flying into Canada exemptions
  • Driving into Canada exemptions
  • Apply for limited (temporary) release from quarantine for compassionate reasons
Entering Canada to care for others, for funerals and for support
  • Non-Canadians: Apply for compassionate entry

  • Canadians and permanent residents: Apply for limited (temporary) release from quarantine for compassionate reasons

Travelling for medical care

If you are in one of these situations, you may be exempt from mandatory quarantine.Being exempt from quarantine does not mean you’re exempt from test requirements.

  • Essential medical services in Canada - a person who enters Canada for the purpose of receiving essential medical services or treatments within 36 hours of entering Canada, other than services or treatments related to COVID-19 as long as they remain under medical supervision for the 14-day period that begins on the day on which they enter Canada.
  • Medical treatments outside Canada - persons who must leave and return to Canada regularly to receive essential medical services in another country with:
      • Written evidence from a licensed health care practitioner in Canada indicating services or treatments outside Canada are essential
    • Written evidence from a licensed health care practitioner in the foreign country indicating services or treatments were provided in that country
Provincial and territorial requirements

If you entered Canada from another country, you are subject to the federal requirements and possible additional provincial or territorial requirements.

In the event of conflicting requirements between federal restrictions to enter Canada and provincial or territorial travel restrictions, you must comply with the regulation that is the most stringent.

If a province or territory has no additional requirements, you will only be subject to the federal requirements after you enter Canada. Requirements are subject to change. Always verify with the province or territory before travelling to Canada.
