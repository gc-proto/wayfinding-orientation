Cross-border students (daily or regular crossing)

Attending school in Canada

Students can only enter Canada if they are attending a designated learning institution (DLI) with a COVID-19 readiness plan approved by its province or territory. If your school has an approved plan, you will likely be exempt from quarantine.

Instructions for cross-border students

Returning to Canada after attending school in the United States

If you’re returning on a daily or regular basis you are likely exempt from quarantine.

If you’re returning occasionally (for example, vacations or holidays) you will not be exempt from quarantine.

You must:

  • read and follow the mandatory quarantine requirements
  • have a suitable place and plan to quarantine

If you’re driving a student

You will likely be exempt from quarantine if the student must regularly attend a school across the border.

You must:

  • go directly to the school, making no other stops along the way
  • only exit your vehicle to drop off or pick up the student and wear a non-medical mask or face covering
If you’re driving a dependent child for custody arrangements

You will likely be exempt from quarantine if you are driving or picking up a dependent child under the terms of a court order regarding custody, access, or parenting.

You must only exit your vehicle to drop off or pick up the child and wear a non-medical mask or face covering.

If a government representative at the border determines you are not quarantine exempt

You must:

  • read and follow the mandatory quarantine requirements
  • make sure you have a suitable place and plan to quarantine

Important legal information upon entry

  • Final determination regarding entry and quarantine is made by a government representative at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time of entry into Canada.
  • You won’t be allowed to board a flight to Canada if you don’t meet the flight requirements.
  • Failure to comply with the COVID-19 Emergency order is an offence under the Quarantine Act and could lead to imprisonment and/or fines.
  • If you’re a dual Canadian citizen, you can only enter Canada with a valid Canadian passport, or special authorization.
