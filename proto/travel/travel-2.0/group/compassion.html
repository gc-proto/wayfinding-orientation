Foreign nationals applying for compassionate entry

All Foreign nationals must apply for and receive written approval for compassionate entry in order to enter Canada. Without this advanced approval, you will likely be denied entry.

You can apply to enter for the following compassionate reasons:

  • to be present during the final moments of life for a loved one
  • to provide support to a person who is critically ill as determined by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada
  • to provide care to a person who has a medical reason as determined by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada
  • to attend a funeral or end of life ceremony

The person you will care for, visit at end of life, or attend the funeral of must reside in Canada and be either:

  • a Canadian citizen
  • a permanent resident of Canada
  • a temporary resident of Canada
  • a protected person (refugee status) or
  • a person registered under the Indian Act

Required documents

Before you can begin your application, you must have the following documents completed and signed, depending on your situation.

Providing support or care for someone, or requesting to be present during the final moments of life

You will require a Letter of Required Support

  1. Download the Letter of Required Support Form (PDF).
  2. Send the letter of required support to the licensed health care professional who can certify that your presence is required.
  3. You'll need the completed and signed letter of required support to submit your completed application to us for processing.

Each applicant requires their own letter of required support.

You may also require a Site Visit Authorization Form if you are applying for a limited (temporary) release of quarantine to care for someone or attend their final moments of life.

Attending a funeral or end of life ceremony

If you need to attend a funeral or end of life ceremony, you will require proof of death. The following documents are examples that can be used as part of the death notification:

  • burial permit
  • death certificate
  • statement of death
  • medical certificate of death
  • letter from the funeral home

You may also require a Site Visit Authorization Form if you are applying for a limited (temporary) release of quarantine to attend.

Limited (temporary) release from quarantine for compassionate reason (required only if you are applying to leave quarantine)

You can request a limited release from quarantine for compassionate reasons as part of your compassionate entry application. You will need additional permission from the site you're visiting.

To be considered for limited release from quarantine a Site Visit Authorization Form is mandatory and requires signature by the person in charge of the location. This can be an administrator, proprietor or homeowner of the site.

A Site Visit Authorization Form isn't required if you're visiting a public outdoor setting or site, for example, a funeral in a public park.

Some provinces and territories may not allow for limited release from quarantine for compassionate reasons. This means that even if you receive approval from the Public Health Agency of Canada a province or territory may have additional restrictions. In the event of conflicting requirements between federal restrictions and provincial or territorial travel restrictions, you must comply with those that is are the most stringent.

Before applying, please check provincial or territorial requirements and check back often to find out if any of their quarantine requirements prohibit limited release from quarantine.

Provincial and territorial travel restrictions

Instructions to request authorization to visit a site

  1. Download the Site Visit Authorization Form (PDF).
  2. Send the Site Visit Authorization Form to the administrator, proprietor or homeowner of each site or location you need to visit during your limited release from quarantine, such as:
    • hospitals
    • funeral home
    • place of worship
    • retirement home
    • long-term care facility
    • palliative care facility, such as a hospice
    • private residence where the death or end of life ceremony is occurring or where you're supporting a critically ill person or caring for a person with a medical condition
  3. You'll need the completed and signed Site Visit Authorization Form(s) to include with your application.

Complete your application

Don't start your application until you have the necessary completed and signed documents:

  • a Letter of Required Support is required if caring for someone or attending the final moments of life
  • a Proof of Death is required to attend a funeral or end of life ceremony
  • a Site Visit Authorization Form is required if you need a limited (temporary) release from your place of quarantine for any compassionate reason

You won't be able to save the form for completion later. Applications will not be accepted with incomplete forms.

Apply for compassionate entry (Traveller Application)

You can apply using the Application for Entry and/or Limited Release from Quarantine Form.

  1. Fill out all required fields on each page of the application.
    • There are 8 pages in total. Clicking 'Next' to get to the following page doesn't submit information or your application.
  2. Attach the signed Letter of Required Support Form and a signed Site Authorization Form(s), if applicable, on page 6 of the application.
  3. Submit your application through the secure portal.
    • You'll receive an immediate message indicating, 'Submission completed successfully.'

You'll also receive a receipt email with an application number.

Your application will be assessed by the Public Health Agency of Canada. We'll confirm that your request meets the requirements for entry and limited release from mandatory quarantine for compassionate reasons.

Once your application is approved

You must bring all required compassionate approvals with you in order to enter Canada. A government representative makes the final decision for entry at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time.

Other types of care

At this time, Canada is not permitting entry to foreign nationals for any other care providing reasons.

Foreign nationals cannot enter Canada to provide:

  • childcare for your relatives

You may still be able to enter Canada to reunite with family.

Canadians entering for compassionate reasons

If you are a Canadian citizen, permanent resident of Canada, person registered under the Indian Act, or a protected person, you are allowed to enter Canada.

Some provinces and territories have entry restrictions in place that could limit your ability to travel to provide care for someone, or attend a funeral in the first 14 days after your arrival in Canada.

Canadians returning after a compassionate visit abroad

If you are a Canadian citizen, permanent resident of Canada, person registered under the Indian Act, or a protected person, you are allowed to enter Canada.

We understand that you left Canada under difficult circumstances, but in order to protect others, you will have to comply with all the same requirements as all other travellers.
