    Starting July 5 at 11:59pm ET: Exemptions for fully vaccinated travellers

    Travel restrictions on who can enter Canada remain in place.

    You may qualify for certain exemptions, if you:

    • are allowed to enter Canada
    • are asymptomatic
    • meet all the criteria of a fully vaccinated traveller

    Those who meet the criteria may have:

    • reduced quarantine with a negative arrive test
    • exemption from the 3-night hotel stopover for air travellers
    • exemption from the day-8 testing requirement

    Fully vaccinated travellers must follow the entry requirements:

    Pre-entry test

    • Getting tested to enter Canada

    Quarantine and hotel stopover

    You must have a suitable quarantine plan in place where you can stay for at least 14 days. If you have symptoms or an unsuitable quarantine plan, you will be asked to follow the instructions provided by the government representative at the border.

    If you meet the criteria of fully vaccinated you must go directly to your place of quarantine to await results of your arrival test

    Air travellers who meet the criteria do not need to stay at a government-authorized hotel to await results of their arrival test.

    Unvaccinated minors or dependent adults who are travelling with fully vaccinated travellers must quarantine for 14 days and follow all testing requirements, but are not required to stay at a government-authorized hotel.

    You may travel to your place of quarantine using:

    • private vehicle
    • onward domestic flight
    • other means of public transportation

    While in transit, you must follow all public health measures, such as wearing a mask.

    Provinces and territories may have their own provincial border measures as well as public health guidelines within their jurisdictions, so it is important that travellers check for the most up-to-date travel requirements for the province or territory of destination.

    Provincial and territorial restrictions

    Arrival testing

    Follow the instructions provided to you upon entry to Canada to complete your arrival (day 1) test. Airports and some land borders have on-site services where you can complete your test before continuing on to your place of quarantine.

    If you receive a home test kit, complete it on the day you arrive and send it to the lab following the instructions provided.

    If your arrival test is negative:

    • your mandatory quarantine period is complete and you may now leave quarantine
    • you may continue to receive emails and notifications from ArriveCAN after you’ve left quarantine, but you don’t need to do daily reports
    • follow local public health measures
    • maintain a list of all close contacts for your first 14 days in Canada
    • keep copies of your proof of vaccination for 14 days

    If your arrival test is positive or someone in your travel party receives a positive test result:

    • do not leave your place of quarantine
    • begin a 14-day isolation period:
      • if your test was done under supervision of the test provider (e.g. video call or on-site at the border), start your isolation on the date you took your test
      • if your test was not supervised by the test provider, start your isolation on the date on the test result
    • keep copies of your proof of vaccination until your isolation period is complete
    • you may continue to receive emails and notifications from ArriveCAN after you’ve left quarantine, but you don’t need to do daily reports
    • you will be contacted by Government of Canada and provincial or territorial officials

    How to meet the full vaccinated exemption

    To be considered fully vaccinated, you must have received the full series of a vaccine – or a combination of vaccines – accepted by the Government of Canada at least 14 days prior to entering Canada.

    You must also use ArriveCAN to enter your vaccination, quarantine and travel information. You may enter this information within 72 hours before to arrival in Canada.

    Authorized vaccines for proof of vaccination

    At this time, Canada is only accepting vaccines authorized by Health Canada. If you received one or a combination of these vaccines outside Canada, it’s still accepted, so long as you received the full dose series. The list of accepted vaccines may expand in the future.

    Authorized vaccines for fully vaccinated status:

    • Pfizer (Comirnaty, tozinameran, BNT162b2)
    • Moderna (mRNA-1273)
    • AstraZeneca (Vaxzevria, AZD1222, Covishield)
    • Janssen (Johnson & Johnson) - single dose

    Vaccines not authorized in Canada:

    • Bharat Biotech (Covaxin, BBV152 A, B, C)
    • Cansino (Convidecia, Ad5-nCoV)
    • Gamalaya (Sputnik V, Gam-Covid-Vac)
    • Sinopharm (BBIBP-CorV, Sinopharm-Wuhan)
    • Sinovac (CoronaVac, PiCoVacc)
    • Vector Institute (EpiVacCorona)
    • Other

    Doses and dates for fully vaccinated status

    To be considered fully vaccinated, you must have:

    • received the full dosage of vaccine at least 14 days before your entry into Canada:
      • 2 doses of any combination of Pfizer, Moderna or AstraZeneca vaccines or
      • one dose of Janssen (Johnson & Johnson) vaccine

    Providing proof of your vaccination

    In ArriveCAN, you must provide:

    • the details of your first dose (date, country and vaccine you received)
    • the details of your second dose if one was required (i.e., for Pfizer, Moderna, and AstraZeneca vaccines)
    • a photo(s) or PDF file(s) of the record of your vaccination(s), such as receipts or confirmations from the vaccinating organization. These must be in English, French, or a certified translation.

    If your proof of vaccination does not meet all of the criteria it may be considered invalid and you may be required to:

    • stay a government-authorized hotel to await the results of your arrival test before continuing on to your place of quarantine
    • complete a quarantine period of at least 14 days
    • complete a test on day 8 of your quarantine

    Final determination of your vaccination status will be made at the border. Bring a copy of your proof(s) of vaccination with you. Additional information on how to enter your vaccination information in ArriveCAN will be available before July 5.

    Partially-vaccinated: No exemptions for partially-vaccinated travellers

    At this time, there are no exemptions from testing, hotel stopover or quarantine for travellers who have only received a single dose of COVID-19 vaccine, other than the Janssen (Johnson and Johnson) vaccine.
