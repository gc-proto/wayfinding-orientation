Read the Privacy Statement

When you apply for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB), your personal information is collected under the authority of the Canada Emergency Response Benefit Act which is being jointly administered by Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) and the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

Your personal information will be used for the purpose of (i) administering your application, and could be used for (ii) issuing a payment to you, (iii) verifying that you meet the program's eligibility requirements, and to (iv) collect any overpayments or erroneous payments that may have been issued to you. Failure to provide information will affect your consideration for benefits.

Please note that to process your application we will need to access information in your tax file. Your information may be shared with ESDC for verification and reporting purposes.

Under the Privacy Act, you have a right of protection, access to and correction of your personal information and to file a complaint with the Privacy Commissioner of Canada regarding our handling of your information.

A personal information bank for this program is under development and will be available in Info Source.

To verify your identity, you'll need

  • your social insurance number (SIN)
  • postal code
Telephone number

Follow the instructions below before you call 1-800-959-2019 (opens up phone application)1-800-959-2019 or 1-800-959-2041 (opens up phone application)1-800-959-2041

Instructions
  1. Select your language preference: English or French
  2. Press '1' to apply for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB)
  3. Follow the prompts to enter your information, including:
    • your SIN
    • confirmation of your postal code
    • the period you are applying for
  4. Declare that you qualify for the benefit

The CRA will issue payments by direct deposit or cheque, based on the payment method we have on file for you.

Both of these services are available 21 hours a day, 7 days a week. Both services are closed from 3:00 am to 6:00 am (Eastern time) for maintenance.

The CERB is a taxable benefit

You will need to report any payments you received on next year's income tax return. A T4A tax slip will be made available for the 2020 tax year in CRA My Account under Tax Information Slips (T4 and more).

When to expect your payment

If you are eligible for the CERB, you can expect to get $2,000 ($500 per week) for the 4-week eligibility period you applied for.

Direct deposit

Direct deposit takes 5-7 business days if you have it set up with the CRA.

By mail

A mailed cheque should arrive in about 10-15 business days. However, in some cases it will take longer for payments to be issued.
