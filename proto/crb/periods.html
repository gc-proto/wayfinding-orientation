How eligibility periods work

Each Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB) eligibility period is a specific 2-week period. It begins and ends on specific dates. When you apply, you will receive an $1,000 ($900 after taxes withheld) payment for the period that you applied for.

The CRB does not renew automatically. You must apply for each period between September 27, 2020 and September 25, 2021 separately. You can apply for a maximum of 13 periods out of the total 26 periods avaiable.

The periods 13 periods you may qualify for do not have to be consecutive.

Which periods you should apply for

You may apply for any eligibility period that you're eligible for. If you meet all the eligibility criteria for the entire 2-week period, the earliest you can apply is the first Monday after the 2-week period has ended.

Periods last 2 weeks

Each eligibility period is a 2-week period with a specific start and end date. You must apply on the Monday after the 2-week period has ended.

You can only apply for 1 period at a time. If your situation continues, you must re-apply for another 2-week period.

Your eligibility ends after 13 periods

You can no longer recieve CRB payments after you have reached the maximum of 13 periods (26 weeks). The benefit is available between September 27, 2020 and September 25, 2021. You may apply for benefits retroactively for any 2-week period within that time frame up to 60 days after that period has ended.

To confirm the number of periods you have already received the CRB, you can review your application history in CRA My Account, under “COVID-19 Support Application Details”.
