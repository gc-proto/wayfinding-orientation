Wage and rent subsidies - Managing your business during COVID-19

Managing your business during COVID-19

Determine your revenue drop

To qualify for and find the amount of your subsidy, you will need to calculate your revenue drop for each claim period.

For periods 22 and later, you also need to know your 12-month average revenue drop. This is a one-time calculation.

On this page

  • What eligible revenue is
  • How the claim period revenue drop is calculated
  • How the 12-month average revenue drop is calculated

What eligible revenue is

Eligible revenue generally includes revenue earned in Canada from:

  • selling goods
  • rendering services, and
  • others' use of your resources

Generally, you should use your normal accounting method when calculating your eligible revenue. If your normal accounting method is the accrual method, you can elect to use the cash method (and vice versa), but once you choose you must use the same accounting method for all your claims.

Note: If you choose to use the cash method, you need to check the “cash method” election box when completing your application.

Read more about calculating eligible revenue

Special revenue circumstances

Registered charities and non-profit organizations

Eligible revenue of registered charities, non-profits, and some prescribed organizations includes donations. These organizations may choose whether or not to include government funding, such as grants, when calculating eligible revenue. Once you’ve chosen, you must use the same approach for each period you are applying for.

Read more about eligible revenue for charities and non-profit organizations in the CEWS technical questions and answers.

Non-arm's-length transactions and affiliated groups

In the CEWS technical questions and answers, you can find special rules for revenue related to:

  • certain non-arm's-length transactions
  • affiliated groups
Amalgamations and changes of ownership

Special rules apply for calculating eligible revenue when two or more employers have amalgamated, or when an employer purchased all (or substantially all) of the assets used by a person or partnership to carry on a business in Canada.

Read more about calculating eligible revenue for amalgamations and changes of ownership in the CEWS technical questions and answers.

How the claim period revenue drop is calculated

You can use the online calculator or spreadsheet to calculate your claim period revenue drop.

Or, read the details of how the claim period revenue drop is calculated.

How the claim period revenue drop is calculated

Your claim period revenue drop is the decline in eligible revenue you experienced when comparing your claim period revenue with your revenue from a specific period of time prior to March 2020 (the “prior reference period”):

Revenue drop (%) = 1 – (Claim period revenue / prior reference period revenue)

Using the current or prior claim period revenue drop (the “deeming rule”)

For all wage and subsidy applications as of July 5, 2020, you calculate two revenue drops and use the higher result in your base rate calculation. If you use the online calculator or spreadsheet to calculate your subsidy amount, both rates will be calculated when you enter your revenues and the higher rate will automatically be applied.

Current period

Revenue drop (%) = 1 – (Current claim period revenue divided by revenue from the prior reference period for the claim period)

or

Previous period

Revenue drop (%) = 1 – (Previous claim period revenue divided by revenue from the prior reference period for the previous claim period)

The higher rate is the one you will use to calculate your subsidy amount. This is your claim period revenue drop. You must repeat this calculation and comparison for each claim period.

Prior reference period options

There are two prior reference period options when calculating your revenue from before the pandemic.

General prior reference period

the eligible revenue you earned in the corresponding month(s) in a specific earlier year

or

Alternative prior reference period

the average of the eligible revenue you earned in January and February, 2020

You must choose one of these prior reference period options and use the same approach when calculating your revenue drop (including any top-up calculations) for all wage and rent subsidy claims that cover July 5, 2020, and later. However:

  • if you were not operating on March 1, 2019, you may elect to use the alternative approach for CEWS claims for periods 14 to 17 and CERS claims for periods 7 to 10, even if you used the general approach for prior claims

Note: If you choose to use the alternative prior reference period (January and February 2020), you need to check the "prior reference period" election box when completing your subsidy application form.

If you were not operating as a business throughout January and February 2020, read more about how to calculate your base revenue drop if you choose the alternative prior reference period in the CEWS technical questions and answers.

How the 12-month average revenue drop is calculated

You only need to calculate the 12-month average revenue drop one time. You will use the same 12-month average revenue drop for all of your claims from 22 onward.

Your 12-month average revenue drop is the average of your revenue drops for each of the first 12 months of the COVID-19 pandemic (March 2020 to February 2021).

Note: If you applied for CEWS periods 1 to 13 or CERS periods 1 to 6, you have already calculated some of the monthly revenue drops you need to find this average.

You can use the calculator to find your 12-month revenue drop.

Or, read the details of how the 12-month average revenue drop is calculated.

How the 12-month average revenue drop is calculated

To get the 12-month average:

If you were operating each month

  1. Calculate your revenue drop for each of the 12 months from March 2020 to February 2021
  2. Add all of the resulting rates together
  3. Divide the result by 12
or

If you were not operating some months

  1. Add up the number of months from March 2020 to February 2021 in which you operated for at least one day
  2. Calculate your revenue drop for each of those months
  3. Add all of the resulting rates together
  4. Divide the result by the number of months in which you were operating

To calculate your revenue drop each month, compare that month's revenue with your revenue from a specific period of time prior to March 2020 (the “prior reference period”):

Revenue drop (%) = 1 − (calculation month revenue ÷ prior reference period revenue)

This is your revenue drop for one month.

Important: if any of the months you calculate had a revenue increase instead of a drop, you must still include those months in your average.

Prior reference period options

Like the calculation for the claim period revenue drop, the same two prior reference period comparison options can be used when calculating your 12-month average, the general approach or the alternative approach.

If you claimed CEWS or CERS from March 2020 to February 2021, you must use the same calculation approach you used when calculating your revenue drop for your claim.

Examples of a 12-month revenue drop calculation

Example: Calculating a 12-month average revenue drop as a seasonal business

Teshia's food truck is normally open from March to October, and does not operate in the winter.

To calculate the 12-month revenue drop, Teshia will:

  1. Total the number of months from March 2020 to February 2021 in which they were operating: 8
  2. Determine the revenue drop for each of the remaining months:
    • March 2020 over March 2019: 95.05%
    • April 2020 over April 2019: 78.33%
    • May 2020 over May 2019: 60.15%
    • June 2020 over June 2019: 51.08%
    • July 2020 over July 2019: -5.05% (increase)
    • August 2020 over August 2019: 42.22%
    • September 2020 over September 2019: 48.83%
    • October 2020 over October 2019: 61.08%
    Teshia chooses the general approach to calculate the revenue drops because they were not operating in January and February of 2020.
  3. Add the drops together: 431.69%
  4. Divide by the number of operational months (8): 53.96%

    5. Teshia's resulting 12-month average revenue drop is 53.96%, which is high enough to meet the 12-month average revenue drop requirement for the THRP or the HHBRP. Teshia can use this result on any subsidy claims they make from period 22 onward, if they meet the other eligibility criteria for that claim period.

Subramaniam's travel agency opened in June 2019. He applied for the CERS as soon as it became available after September 27, 2020.
