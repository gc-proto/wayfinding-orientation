Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP)

The THRP provides wage and rent support to Canadian businesses, charities, and non-profits that have been affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. It includes the Local Lockdown Program.

Eligible organizations may qualify if they are either:

  • part of the tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment or recreation sectors, or
  • affected by a local lockdown resulting from a public health restriction

Eligibility for the THRP (includes Local Lockdown Program)

There are two ways eligible organizations can qualify for the THRP:

Tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment, and recreation sectors: Option 1 of 2

  • You are in one of the specific tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment, or recreation sectors this program supports
  • You have a 12-month average revenue drop from March 2020 to February 2021 of at least 40%
  • You have a claim period revenue drop of at least 40%

Find out if your sector is considered part of tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment, or recreation

Organizations affected by a local lockdown resulting from a public health restriction : Option 2 of 2

This support is part of the THRP and was introduced as the Local Lockdown Program. It is available to eligible organizations within any business sector.

  • You were affected by a local lockdown caused by a qualifying public health restriction (local lockdown)
  • You have a claim period revenue drop of at least 40%

Qualifying tourism or hospitality entities

If you primarily operate within one of the specific tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment, or recreation sectors listed, you are considered a qualifying tourism or hospitality entity for the purposes of being eligible for the THRP.

To qualify as part of one of these sectors, at least 50% of your revenue from a specific period before the COVID-19 pandemic must have come from one or a combination of the qualifying sectors. The months you include when calculating your revenue from these qualifying sectors are the same as the prior reference period months (applicable months between March 2019 and February 2020) you use to calculate your 12-month average revenue drop.

Qualifying public health restriction

If your business, charity or non-profit has at least 1 location that is affected by a qualifying public health restriction (local lockdown), you may be eligible for the THRP even if you are not a qualifying tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment, or recreation entity.

A qualifying public health restriction means that:

  • you were affected by a public health restriction that was in effect for at least 7 days in the claim period
  • your affected activities accounted for at least approximately 25% of your total business revenue (not only at the affected locations) during the prior reference period for the claim period
    • the affected locations) during the prior reference period for the claim period

A public health restriction must:

  • be issued by a federal, provincial, or municipal government, or a local health authority
  • be limited based on at least one of these factors:
    • geographical boundaries
    • type of business or other activity
    • risks associated with a particular location
  • result in sanctions or be an offence if you do not comply
  • require you or your non-arm’s-length tenant that has a business at the qualifying property to stop some or all of your regular business activities at the qualifying property for at least 7 days in a row
Example – Kim's tailor shop

Kim owns a tailor shop. They had to close twice recently due to public health restriction.

Dates closed:

  1. October 18 to October 27, 2021, inclusive (10 days in a row), and
  2. November 8 to November 14, 2021, inclusive (7 days in a row)

Claim period 22:

  • started on October 24, 2021, and
  • ended on November 10, 2021

This means that Kim’s shop was closed for a total of 7 days during claim period 22:

  • 4 days at the beginning of period 22 (October 24 to 27, inclusive), and
  • 3 days at the end of period 22 (November 8 to 10, inclusive)

Assume their affected activities accounted for at least approximately 25% of their total revenue during the prior reference period.

Kim’s tailor shop may be eligible for the THRP for claim period 22 because they were affected by a qualifying public health restriction.

They must meet all other eligibility criteria before they apply.

To be eligible to apply for THRP if you are affected by a qualifying public health restriction, you:

  • must have a 40% revenue drop for the current claim period when compared to the corresponding prior reference period
  • do not need a 12-month average revenue drop

Organizations eligible for the THRP and HHBRP

Types of entities who were eligible for the CRHP, CEWS, or CERS will generally be eligible for the THRP and HHBRP.

This means:

  • You had a CRA payroll account on March 15, 2020 (exceptions apply in payroll provider and asset acquisition situations), or, for rent portions of the subsidy only, you had a business number (BN) with CRA as of September 27, 2020.
  • You are one of the following types eligible organizations:
    • individuals (such as sole proprietors)
    • corporations (or trusts) that are not exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act)
    • the following persons that are exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act):
      • non-profit organizations
      • agricultural organizations
      • boards of trade
      • chambers of commerce
      • non-profit corporations for scientific research and experimental development
      • labour organizations or societies
      • benevolent or fraternal benefit societies or orders
    • registered charities
    • partnerships consisting of eligible entities
    • the following prescribed organizations:
      • certain Indigenous government-owned corporations that carry on a business
      • partnerships consisting of eligible entities and certain Indigenous governments
      • registered Canadian amateur athletic associations
      • registered journalism organizations
      • private schools or private colleges, and
      • partnerships consisting of eligible entities (including partnerships where at least 50% of the interests in the partnership are held by eligible entities)

      Public institutions are not eligible for the subsidy. This includes municipalities and local governments, Crown corporations, public universities, colleges and schools, and hospitals.

    Read more about who is an eligible entity in the CEWS technical questions and answers.

12-month average revenue drop

Each claim period, you will need to know how much your eligible revenue has dropped since before the pandemic.

For the THRP and HHBRP (periods 22 and later), you also need to know the average of all of the applicable revenue drops and increases you experienced from March 2020 to February 2019 (the 12-month average revenue drop). This is a one-time calculation.

You can:

  • calculate your 12-month average revenue drop online, or
  • read the details of how the 12-month average revenue drop is calculated.

Claim period revenue drop

The existing rules continue to apply for the purposes of calculating the claim period revenue drop for the wage and rent subsidies.

Subsidies you may qualify for under the THRP

Through the THRP, whether you qualify as part of a tourism or hospitality sector or because you were subject to a public health restriction, you may be able to get a subsidy to help cover wages (formerly the Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy) and rent (formerly the Canada Emergency Rent Subsidy).

Wage and hiring

The wage or hiring subsidy will enable you to re-hire workers previously laid off as a result of COVID-19, help prevent further job losses, and better position you to resume normal operations following the crisis.
