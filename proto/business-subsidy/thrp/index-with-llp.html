Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP)

The Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP) provides wage and rent support to Canadian businesses, charities, and non-profits that have been affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. These organizations may qualify either as:

  • part of the heavily-affected tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment or recreation industries
  • any industry affected by a local lockdown resulting from a public health restriction Also called: Local Lockdown Program (LLP)

Who can apply for the THRP

There are two ways eligible organizations can qualify for the THRP:

Tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment, and recreation sectors: Option 1 of 2

Organizations may qualify either as part of the heavily-affected tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment, or recreation industries

Refer to: Eligibility criteria situation 1

Any business affected by a public health restriction causing a local lockdown : Option 2 of 2 Also called: Local Lockdown Program (LLP)

The LLP functions within the Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP), but applies to sectors outside the tourism, hospitality, arts, entertainment, and recreation sectors.

This subsidy will be available to all businesses, within any business sector.

Refer to: Eligibility criteria situation 2

    Qualifying tourism or hospitality entities

    If you primarily operate within one of the specific tourism, arts, entertainment, and recreation industries listed, you are considered a qualifying tourism or hospitality entity for the purposes of being eligible for the THRP.

    To qualify as part of one of these industries, at least 50% of your revenue from a specific period before the COVID-19 pandemic must have come from one or a combination of the qualifying industries. The months you include when calculating your revenue from these qualifying industries are the same as the prior reference period months (applicable months between March 2019 and February 2020) you use to calculate your 12-month average revenue drop.

Situation 2
Any business affected by a public health restriction causing a local lockdown Also called: Local Lockdown Program (LLP)

  • You were affected by a local lokdown cause by a qualifying public health restriction that affected at least 1 location for 7 days or more within the claim period
    Qualifying local lockdown criteria

    Qualifying public health restriction criteria

    If your business, charity or non-profit has at least 1 location that is affected by a qualifying public health restriction that resulted in a lockdown, you may be eligible for the THRP through the local lockdown component, even if you are not a qualifying tourism or hospitality entity.

    The local lockdown component allows businesses, charities and non-profits that are not qualifying tourism or hospitality entities to apply for the THRP.

    This means:

    • There was a COVID-19-related public health order (lockdown) in effect during the claim period that required you to close or stop certain business activities at 1 or more locations
    • The public health order was in effect for at least 7 days in the claim period
    • You needed to close or stop certain business activities during the public health order at 1 or more locations
    • The affected activities accounted for at least approximately 25% of your total revenue (not only at the affected locations) during the prior reference period for the claim period

    To be eligible for the THRP due to a qualifying public health restriction that resulted in a lockdown, you:

    • must have a 40% revenue drop for the current claim period when compared to the corresponding prior reference period
    • do not need a 12-month average revenue drop
    Example: of a qualifying local lockdown

    Kim owns a tailor shop. They had to close twice recently due to a COVID-related public health order causing a lockdown.

    Dates closed:

    1. October 18 to October 27, 2021, inclusive, and
    2. November 8 to November 14, 2021, inclusive

    Claim period 22:

    • started on October 24, 2021, and
    • ended on November 10, 2021

    This means that Kim’s shop was closed for a total of 7 days during claim period 22:

    • 4 days at the beginning of period 22 (October 24 to 27, inclusive), and
    • 3 days at the end of period 22 (November 8 to 10, inclusive)

    Because of this, Kim’s tailor shop may be eligible for the Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP) for claim period 22. Even though they are not a qualifying tourism or hospitality business, they may qualify through the local lockdown component.

    They must have at least a 40% current period revenue drop and meet all other eligibility criteria before they apply.

    A COVID-19-related local lockdown public health order must:

    • be issued by a federal, provincial, or municipal government, or a local health authority
    • be limited based on at least 1 of these factors:
      • geographic boundaries
      • type of business or other activity
      • risks associated with a particular location
    • result in sanctions or be an offence if you do not comply
    • require you to stop some or all of your regular activities while the order is in place
  • The public health restriction prevented activities that account for at least 25% of your business's revenues
  • You have a claim period revenue drop of at least 40%

Organizations eligible for the THRP and HHBRP

Types of entities who were eligible for the CRHP, CEWS, or CERS will generally be eligible for the THRP and HHBRP.

This means:

  • You had a CRA payroll account on March 15, 2020 (exceptions apply in payroll provider and asset acquisition situations), or, for rent portions of the subsidy only, you had a business number (BN) with CRA as of September 27, 2020.
  • You are one of the following types eligible organizations:
    • individuals (such as sole proprietors)
    • corporations (or trusts) that are not exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act)
    • the following persons that are exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act):
      • non-profit organizations
      • agricultural organizations
      • boards of trade
      • chambers of commerce
      • non-profit corporations for scientific research and experimental development
      • labour organizations or societies
      • benevolent or fraternal benefit societies or orders
    • registered charities
    • partnerships consisting of eligible entities
    • the following prescribed organizations:
      • certain Indigenous government-owned corporations that carry on a business
      • partnerships consisting of eligible entities and certain Indigenous governments
      • registered Canadian amateur athletic associations
      • registered journalism organizations
      • private schools or private colleges, and
      • partnerships consisting of eligible entities (including partnerships where at least 50% of the interests in the partnership are held by eligible entities)

      Public institutions are not eligible for the subsidy. This includes municipalities and local governments, Crown corporations, public universities, colleges and schools, and hospitals.

    Read more about who is an eligible entity in the CEWS technical questions and answers.

12-month average revenue drop

Each claim period, you will need to know how much your eligible revenue has dropped since before the pandemic.

For the THRP and HHBRP (periods 22 and later), you also need to know the average of all of the applicable revenue drops and increases you experienced from March 2020 to February 2019 (the 12-month average revenue drop). This is a one-time calculation.

You can:

  • calculate your 12-month average revenue drop online, or
  • read the details of how the 12-month average revenue drop is calculated.

Claim period revenue drop

The existing rules continue to apply for the purposes of calculating the claim period revenue drop for the wage and rent subsidies.

Subsidies you may qualify for under the THRP

Through the THRP, you may be able to get a subsidy to help cover wages (formerly the Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy) and rent (formerly the Canada Emergency Rent Subsidy).

Wage and hiring

The wage subsidy will enable you to re-hire workers previously laid off as a result of COVID-19, help prevent further job losses, and better position you to resume normal operations following the crisis.
