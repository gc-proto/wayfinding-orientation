How the wage subsidies have changed

The CEWS ended effective October 23, 2021 and was replaced by 2 new programs.

You may still apply for the claim periods that are still open, but there are no new periods after CEWS claim period 21.

The CRHP has been extended to May 7, 2022.

Starting on October 24, 2021, you may qualify for wage support through the:

  • Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP) or the
  • Hardest-Hit Business Recovery Program (HHBRP) or the
  • Canada Recovery Hiring Program

If your business activities are significantly affected by a qualifying public health restriction, you may be eligible for support through the THRP even if you are not a tourism or hospitality business.

What is a qualifying public health restriction? [Pop-up definition TBD]

Many of the THRP and HHBRP rules about who can apply, which employees you can include, and how to calculate the subsidy are the same as they were for the CEWS.

What wage subsidies you may apply for

If you are an eligible employer and have qualifying wage and payroll expenses, you may be eligible to apply for one of the wage subsidies.

Example

Your business has eligible wage expenses. Your business also has hiring expenses that are eligible for CRHP.

You:

  • are not a part of the tourism and hospitality industry
  • have not been affected by a qualifying lockdown
  • qualify as a hardest-hit business under the HHBRP

When you calculate your amounts for the wage subsidy:

  • if the CRHP offers a higher amount than the HHBRP, you may apply for the CRHP instead of the HHBRP wage portion
  • if the HHBRP offers a higher amount than the CRHP, you may apply for the HHBRP wage portion instead of the CRHP

The THRP and the HHBRP also include a rent and mortgage subsidy portion.
