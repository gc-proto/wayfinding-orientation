Eligibility criteria

To be eligible to receive a wage subsidy, you must meet both of the following criteria:

  1. You had a CRA payroll account on March 15, 2020

    Even if you didn’t have a payroll account on March 15, you may still qualify if:

    Situation 1

    another person or partnership made remittances on your behalf

    or

    Situation 2

    you purchased all (or almost all) of another person's or partnership’s business assets

  2. You are one of the following types of employers

    • individuals (such as sole proprietors)
    • corporations (or trusts) that are not exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act)
    • the following persons that are exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act):
      • non-profit organizations
      • agricultural organizations
      • boards of trade
      • chambers of commerce
      • non-profit corporations for scientific research and experimental development
      • labour organizations or societies
      • benevolent or fraternal benefit societies or orders
    • registered charities
    • partnerships consisting of eligible employers
    • the following prescribed organizations:
      • certain Indigenous government-owned corporations that carry on a business
      • partnerships consisting of eligible employers and certain Indigenous governments
      • registered Canadian amateur athletic associations
      • registered journalism organizations
      • private schools or private colleges, and
      • partnerships consisting of eligible employers (including partnerships where at least 50% of the interests in the partnership are held by eligible employers)

    Public institutions are not eligible for the subsidy. This includes municipalities and local governments, Crown corporations, public universities, colleges and schools, and hospitals.

    Read more about which types of employers are eligible in the CEWS technical questions and answers.

Additional requirements for specific programs and subsidies

For claim periods 22 and later:

  • answer a few questions to find out which subsidies you are eligible for

Or, for all claim periods, read the additional requirements to be eligible for the specific programs and subsidies:

Tourism and Hospitality Industry Recovery Program (THRP) eligibility

There are two ways eligible organizations can qualify for the THRP:

Tourism and hospitality sector: Option 1 of 2

  • You are part of the tourism and hospitality industry
  • You have a 12-month average revenue drop from March 2020 to February 2021 of at least 40%
  • You have a claim period revenue drop of at least 40%

All sectors: Option 2 of 2

You do not need to be part of the tourism and hospitality sector to qualify if:

  • You were affected by a qualifying public health restriction that affected at least one location for 7 days or more within the claim period
  • The public health restriction prevented activities that account for at least 25% of your business's revenues
  • You have a claim period revenue drop of at least 40%
Hardest-Hit Businesses Recovery Program (HHBRP) eligibility

Eligible organizations can qualify for the HHBRP by meeting the following two conditions:

  • You have a 12-month average revenue drop from March 2020 to February 2021 of at least 50%
  • You have a claim period revenue drop of at least 50%
Canada Recovery Hiring Program (CRHP) eligibility

Types of employers eligible for CRHP

All types of employers who are eligible for the wage subsidy are eligible for CRHP, except some for-profit corporations and partnerships.

If you are a for-profit corporation, you must be either:

  • a Canadian-controlled private corporations (CCPC), or
  • a cooperative corporations that is eligible for the small business deduction

If you are a partnership, at least 50% of the partnership’s interests must be held (directly or indirectly) by employers eligible for the CRHP.

Claim period revenue drop required

Period 17
No minimum revenue drop required
Periods 18 and later
Minimum revenue drop of 10% required
Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS) eligibility

There is a minimum claim period revenue drop required to qualify for a CEWS amount for some claim periods:

Periods 5 to 17
No minimum revenue drop required
Periods 18 and later
Minimum revenue drop of more than 10% required to qualify for the subsidy for active employees, although you may still qualify for a CEWS amount for your employees on leave with pay if your revenue drop is 10% or less

If you need information about qualifying for the CEWS prior to July 2020, refer to the CEWS technical questions and answers.

Revenue drop requirements

Each claim period, you will need to know how much your eligible revenue has dropped since before the pandemic.

For the THRP and HHBRP (periods 22 and later), you also need to know your 12-month average revenue drop. This is a one-time calculation.

You can:

  • use the online calculator or spreadsheet to calculate your claim period revenue drop as part of your subsidy calculation
  • calculate your 12-month revenue drop online, or
  • read the details of what eligible revenue is and how the revenue drops are calculated

Confirming your eligibility

If we notice a mistake in your application, we will work with you to correct it. If you realize you made a mistake in your application, you can request a change to your previous claim.

We will review your claim and confirm the information you submit.

Qualifying public health restriction for the THRP

If your business or organization has at least 1 location that is affected by a qualifying public health restriction, you may be eligible for the Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP) even if you are not a qualifying tourism or hospitality entity.

This means:

  • There was a COVID-19-related public health order in effect during the claim period which required you to close or stop certain business activities at 1 or more locations
  • The public health order was in effect for at least 7 days in the claim period
  • You needed to close or stop certain business activities during the public health order at 1 or more locations
  • The affected activities accounted for at least approximately 25% of your total revenue (not just at the affected locations) during the prior reference period for the claim period

A COVID-19-related public health order must:

  • be issued by a federal, provincial, or municipal government, or a local health authority
  • be limited based on at least 1 of these factors:
    • geographic boundaries
    • type of business or other activity
    • risks associated with a particular location
  • result in sanctions or be an offence if you do not comply
  • require you to stop some or all of your regular activities while the order is in place

To be eligible for THRP due to a qualifying public health restriction, you:

  • must have a 40% revenue drop for the current claim period when compared to the corresponding prior reference period
  • do not need a 12-month average revenue drop
Example – Qualifying public health restriction

Kim owns a tailor shop. They were recently closed 2 times due to a COVID-related public health order:

  1. October 18 to October 27, 2021, inclusive, and
  2. November 8 to November 14, 2021, inclusive

Claim period 22:

  • started on October 24, 2021, and
  • ended on November 10, 2021

This means that Kim’s shop was closed for a total of 7 days during claim period 22:

  • 4 days at the beginning of period 22 (October 24 to 27 inclusive), and
  • 3 days at the end of period 22 (November 8 to 10, inclusive)

Because of this, Kim’s tailor shop may be eligible to apply for the Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program for claim period 22 even though they are not a qualifying tourism or hospitality business. They must have at least a 40% current period revenue drop and meet all other eligibility criteria before they apply.
