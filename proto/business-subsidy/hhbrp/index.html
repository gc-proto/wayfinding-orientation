Hardest-Hit Business Recovery Program (HHBRP)

As a Canadian business, charity, or non-profit who has faced deep losses due to the COVID-19 pandemic, you may be eligible for wage or rent subsidies through the HHBRP.

Who can apply for the HHBRP

Eligible organizations, regardless of their business sector, can qualify for the HHBRP by meeting the following two conditions:

  • You have a 12-month average revenue drop from March 2020 to February 2021 of at least 50%
  • You have a claim period revenue drop of at least 50%

Eligibility for COVID-19 business subsidies

There are different ways to qualify for wage or rent subsidies if you've been affected by the COVID-19 pandemic, such as being affected by a qualifying public health restriction or as part of the tourism or hospitality sectors. If you're not sure which subsidy may fit your situation:

  • Answer a few questions to see what you qualify for

Subsidies you may qualify for under the HHBRP

Through the HHBRP, you may be able to get a subsidy to help cover wages (formerly the Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy) and rent (formerly the Canada Emergency Rent Subsidy).

Wage and hiring

The wage or hiring subsidy will enable you to re-hire workers previously laid off as a result of COVID-19, help prevent further job losses, and better position you to resume normal operations following the crisis.

Rent and property

The rent subsidy covers part of commercial rent or property expenses and will be provided directly to the qualifying renter or property owner, not the landlord.

Organizations eligible for the THRP and HHBRP

Types of entities who were eligible for the CRHP, CEWS, or CERS will generally be eligible for the THRP and HHBRP.

This means that to be eligible to apply, you must:

  • have had a CRA payroll account on March 15, 2020 (exceptions apply in payroll provider and asset acquisition situations), or, for rent portions of the subsidy only, you had a business number (BN) with CRA as of September 27, 2020.
  • be one of the following types eligible entities:
    • individuals (such as sole proprietors)
    • corporations (or trusts) that are not exempt from income tax under Part I of the Income Tax Act
    • the following persons that are exempt from income tax under Part I of the Income Tax Act:
      • non-profit organizations
      • agricultural organizations
      • boards of trade
      • chambers of commerce
      • non-profit corporations for scientific research and experimental development
      • labour organizations or societies
      • benevolent or fraternal benefit societies or orders
    • registered charities
    • partnerships consisting of eligible entities
    • the following prescribed organizations:
      • certain Indigenous government-owned corporations that carry on a business
      • partnerships consisting of eligible entities and certain Indigenous governments
      • registered Canadian amateur athletic associations
      • registered journalism organizations
      • private schools or private colleges, and
      • partnerships consisting of eligible entities (including partnerships where at least 50% of the interests in the partnership are held by eligible entities)

      Public institutions are not eligible for the subsidy. This includes municipalities and local governments, Crown corporations, public universities, colleges and schools, and hospitals.

    Read more about who is an eligible entity in the CEWS technical questions and answers.

12-month average revenue drop

Each claim period, you will need to know how much your eligible revenue has dropped since before the pandemic.

For the THRP and HHBRP (periods 22 and later), you also need to know the average of all of the applicable revenue drops and increases you experienced from March 2020 to February 2019 (CEWS claim periods 1 to 13, excluding either period 10 or 11). This is a one-time calculation.

You can:

  • calculate your 12-month average revenue drop online, or
  • read the details of how the 12-month average revenue drop is calculated.

Claim period revenue drop

The existing rules continue to apply for the purposes of calculating the claim period revenue drop for the wage and rent portions of the THRP and HHBRP.
