Why you may want to change or cancel a claim

You may need or want to adjust or cancel an application you've already submitted if:

  • you made a mistake on your application
  • you realize you were not eligible for the claim period
  • the subsidy rules changed or were clarified after you submitted your application

You will need to make sure you continue to meet eligibility requirements for any updated claims, and keep records supporting your wage subsidy claim adjustment.

How to change or cancel a claim you submitted

You may request adjustments or cancel applications you have already submitted. To change a previous wage subsidy claim, visit:

  • My Business Account, or
  • Represent a Client

If you applied using the Web Forms application and need to change your claim, you may call CRA’s business enquiries phone number.

When completing the adjustment, you will need:

  • your payroll program account number (123456789RP0001, for example)
  • to know which claim period you are adjusting
  • all of the information necessary to change the amount on each line(s) you would like to adjust

To cancel an application, change the amount on each line to zero.

Representatives who are changing a claim on behalf of an employer must be authorized at level 2 or 3. You will need to have an updated Attestation for owner/managers and/or senior employees to support the amended application.

You may need to return all or part of the subsidy you have already received if you change or cancel an application for that claim period.

Disagreeing with a decision about your application

If you disagree with the decision made by the CRA about your wage subsidy claim, you may request a second-level review of your application by signing into My Business Account and selecting “Register a formal dispute”.

Your request must:

  • include all supporting documents
  • be submitted within 30 days of the date of the letter that communicated CRA’s original decision

The second-level review is conducted by someone other than the original decision maker.

If you disagree with the second-level review decision, you may seek formal recourse through the appeals process once a Notice of Assessment or Notice of Determination has been issued for your income tax return for the tax year in which the claim period ends.
