Why you would send CWLB back

If you are not eligible for the Canada Recovery Benefit (CWLB), you will have to return any payments you received. Review CWLB period start and end dates in a calendar.

Return or repay the CWLB to the CRA if you:

  • applied for the CWLB and later found that you do not meet the eligibility criteria
  • received a payment in error
  • did not file an income tax return for the year you received your benefit payments
    • Tax filing requirements for this benefit

    For CWLB payments received in:

    • 2021 - you must file your 2021 taxes by December 31, 2022
    • 2022 - you must file your 2021 and 2022 taxes by December 31, 2023

    We recommend you file your tax return by the filing due date.

Individuals who make intentionally fraudulent claims may also face additional consequences, such as penalties or possible jail time.

How to return CWLB to CRA

You can repay your CWLB payment to the CRA online, by mail, or in person.

Pay online

CRA My Account:

  • Interac Debit
  • Visa Debit
  • Debit Mastercard
How to pay online with your CRA My Account

After you sign into your CRA My Account,

  1. Locate COVID-19 Support
  2. Select Send the money back, in the right column, to start your emergency support repayment

Keep a copy of your transaction receipt for your records.

Sign in to CRA My Account

CRA My Payment:

  • Interac Debit
  • Visa Debit
  • Debit Mastercard
How to pay online with CRA My Payment

CRA My Payment lets you directly pay the CRA with your bank card.

On Pay now with My Payment ,

  1. Select the Pay now button to start
  2. In Step 1, select Individual income tax (T1) as your payment type
  3. Then select Canada emergency benefit repayment as your payment allocation and follow the instructions

Keep a copy of your transaction receipt for your records.

Online banking:

  • Canadian financial institutions
How to pay using your online banking service

Sign in to your financial institution’s online service,

  1. Under "Add a payee", look for an option such as Canada emergency benefit repayment or CEB repayment to repay a CRA-administered COVID-19 benefit
  2. Enter your 9-digit social insurance number (SIN) as the CRA account number

Other online services:

  • Credit card
  • Debit card
  • PayPal
  • Interac e-Transfer
How to pay online with PaySimply

PaySimply is a CRA third-party service provider (service fees apply).

On the PaySimply welcome page,

  1. Select Canada Emergency Benefit Repayment to start
  2. Enter your payer details and follow the instructions to complete the payment process
Pay by mail

Send a payment by mail:

  • Cheque
  • Money order
  • Cash not accepted

How to pay from your Canadian bank account

Send a cheque or money order. Cash is not accepted.

  1. Make the cheque or money order payable to Receiver General for Canada
  2. Indicate the name of the benefit you are repaying
  3. Indicate which eligibility period you are repaying
  4. Include your Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  5. Mail the cheque or money order to:
    • Revenue Processing – Repayment of CEB
      Sudbury Tax Centre
      1050 Notre Dame Avenue
      Sudbury ON P3A 0C3

If your payment is not honoured, the CRA will charge a fee.

If you have the original cheque, you may return it by mail.

Be sure to include:

  1. The reason for returning the cheque ("not entitled" or "overpayment")
  2. Your Social Insurance Number (SIN)
Pay in person

Visit Canada Post:

  • Debit card
  • Cash

How to pay at a Canada Post location with a QR code

Create a QR code and visit a Canada Post location (service fees apply)

  1. Create a QR code
  2. Bring your code to a Canada Post outlet to make your payment

How repayments can impact your taxes

If you repaid benefit amounts:

  • before December 31, 2021 - repayments should be reflected on your 2021 T4A slip (you won’t pay tax on repaid amounts on your 2021 taxes)
  • after December 31, 2021 - you will be taxed based on the full amount shown on your 2022 T4A slip (repayments will be reported on your 2022 T4A slip and claimed on next year's tax return as a deduction)

If any amounts are incorrect, they can be corrected by calling the CRA. Follow the instructions under Resolve issues with your T4A slip.
