Who can authorize a lockdown
  • the government of Canada
  • the government of a province or territory
  • a municipality
  • a public health authority
  • a council, government or other entity who is authorized to act on behalf of an Indigenous group, community, or people
What is considered a lockdown

Counts as a lockdown:

  • a public health order due to COVID-19 is made by a provincial or territorial, municipality, or regional health authority under the laws of Canada
  • limited to a specific geographical boundary
  • closure of non-essential businesses and services to the public for at least 14 consecutive days
  • requirement to stay at home unless to go out for essential services (e.g. groceries, health emergencies)
  • Not following the rules of a lockdown order is an offence which may result in a penalty.
