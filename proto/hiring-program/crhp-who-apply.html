Eligibility criteria

Employers who are eligible for the CEWS are eligible for the CRHP, except for some corporations and partnerships.

This means, to be eligible to receive the CRHP, you must meet all three of the following criteria:

  1. Have had a CRA payroll account on March 15, 2020

    Even if you didn’t have a payroll account on March 15, you may still qualify if:

    Situation 1

    another person or partnership made remittances on your behalf

    or

    Situation 2

    you purchased all (or almost all) of another person's or partnership’s business assets

  2. Be one of the following types of employers

    Employers eligible for CRHP and CEWS

    • individuals (such as sole proprietors)
    • registered charities

    The following persons that are exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act):

    • non-profit organizations
    • agricultural organizations
    • boards of trade
    • chambers of commerce
    • non-profit corporations for scientific research and experimental development
    • labour organizations or societies
    • benevolent or fraternal benefit societies or orders

    The following prescribed organizations:

    • certain Indigenous government-owned corporations that carry on a business
    • partnerships consisting of eligible employers and certain Indigenous governments
    • registered Canadian amateur athletic associations
    • registered journalism organizations
    • private schools or private colleges, and
    • partnerships consisting of eligible employers (including partnerships where at least 50% of the interests in the partnership are held by eligible employers)
    or

    For-profit corporate and partnership employers (special eligibility for CRHP)

    • Canadian-controlled private corporations (CCPCs)
    • cooperative corporations that are eligible for the small business deduction
    • partnerships where at least 50% of the partnership’s interests are held (directly or indirectly) by employers eligible for the CRHP

    Employers that are not eligible for the CRHP

    • Public institutions are not eligible for the subsidy. This includes municipalities and local governments, Crown corporations, public universities, colleges and schools, and hospitals.

    • Any for-profit corporations that are not CCPCs and not cooperatives eligible for the small business deduction are not eligible for the CRHP, though they may still be eligible for the CEWS
    • Partnerships where at least 50% of the partnership’s interests are held (directly or indirectly) by employers are not eligible for the CRHP

    These corporations and partnerships may still be eligible for the CEWS, just not the CRHP.

  3. Have experienced a drop in revenue

    The CRHP revenue drop calculation and requirement for each claim period is the same as for the CEWS.

    Your drop in revenue is calculated by comparing your eligible revenue during the crisis with your eligible revenue from a previous period (baseline revenue).

    For claim period 17 (claim that covers June 6 to July 3, 2021), there is no minimum revenue drop required to qualify for the CRHP.

    For claim periods 18 to 22 (claims that cover July 4 to November 20, 2021), you must have had a revenue drop of more than 10% to qualify for the subsidy for the CRHP.

    • Use the online calculator or downloadable spreadsheet to find your amount
    • See the math behind how the subsidy is calculated

    Read about the calculation

    You can read the in-depth details of how the revenue drop and subsidy rate are calculated at the CEWS website.

Confirming your eligibility

If we notice a mistake in your application, we will work with you to correct it. If you realize you made a mistake in your application, you can request a change to your previous claim.

We will review your claim and confirm the information you submit.
