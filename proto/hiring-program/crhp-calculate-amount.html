Calculate your amount

Use the online calculator or downloadable spreadsheet before you apply to find the amount of CRHP or CEWS you can claim.

  1. Enter your revenue and employee pay information into the calculator
  2. The calculator will show you whether the CRHP or CEWS amount is higher, so you will know which subsidy to apply for
  3. Print and save the numbers you'll need to enter when you apply
  4. Apply for either CRHP or CEWS online through My Business Account, Represent a Client, or the Web Forms application using the information you printed

  Calculate your subsidy amount

See the math behind the calculation

For each claim (qualifying) period, the amount of CRHP equals (A − B) × C, where:

  • A
    The total amount of eligible remuneration you paid to all your active eligible employees during the claim period. For each eligible employee, for each week of the claim period, the amount is the least of the following:
    • the amount of eligible remuneration paid to each employee in respect of that week
    • $1129, or
    • if the employee is a non-arm’s-length employee, the pre-crisis remuneration of the employee determined for that week
  • minus B
    The total amount of eligible remuneration you paid to all your active eligible employees during the base period (March 14 to April 10, 2021 — CEWS period 14). For each eligible employee, for each week of the claim period, the amount is the least of the following:
    • the amount of eligible remuneration paid to each employee in respect of that week
    • $1129, or
    • if the employee is a non-arm’s-length employee, the pre-crisis remuneration of the employee determined for that week
  • equals
    Increase in employee pay (incremental remuneration)
  • muliplied by C
    Flat rate for the claim period you are applying for
    • Subsidy rate Claim period
      50% 17 (June 6 to July 3, 2021)
      50% 18 (July 4 to July 31, 2021)
      50% 19 (August 1 to August 28, 2021)
      40% 20 (August 29 to September 25, 2021)
      30% 21 (September 26 to October 23, 2021)
      20% 22 (October 23 to November 20, 2021)

There is no overall limit on the total subsidy amount that an eligible employer may claim.
