Securing potential vaccines and supplies

The Government of Canada is securing millions of doses of potential vaccine candidates. All potential vaccines will require Health Canada authorization prior to being used to vaccinate Canadians. Clinical human trials in phases help ensure that potential COVID-19 vaccines are safe and effective.

Planning for manufacturing and immunization

Plans and purchases are being made to efficiently, effectively and equitably produce, distribute and administer an eventual COVID-19 vaccine. These include:

  • Accelerate production and testing of vaccine candidates by the National Research Council
  • Agreements to purchase syringes, alcohol swabs and other immunization supplies
  • Funding and agreements for final manufacturing and packing stages of vaccine production in Canada
  • Assess feasibility and equity of options for storage and handling and administration

Supported by: Public Services and Procurement Canada, Innovation, Science and Economic Development, the COVID-19 Vaccine Task Force and, the COVID-19 Therapeutics Task Force.

Vaccine progress in Canada

All drugs, including vaccines, are evaluated by Health Canada's scientific reviewers to assess the potential benefits and risks. Products must meet Health Canada's requirements for safety, efficacy and quality before they can be used in Canada. Human trials for some vaccines are taking place in Canada.

Vaccines authorized for use in Canada:none

Health Canada is closely tracking all potential vaccines in development. Authorization processes may begin during clinical trials in other countris, as well as in Canada.

Drugs and treatment progress in Canada

There are many potential drugs being investigated in Canada and around the world for use against COVID-19. Drugs must meet the regulatory requirements for safety, efficacy and quality before they are authorized for use in Canada.

Drugs authorized for the treatment of COVID-19

  • Veklury - Remdesivir solution for injection

Clinical trials of drugs in Canada

Potential COVID-19 drugs currently being investigated in authorized Canadian clinical trials include:

  • antiviral drugs
  • anti-malarial drugs
  • monoclonal antibodies and other drugs that address inflammation
  • convalescent plasma collected from patients who have recovered from COVID-19
  • medical gases

See the complete list of authorized COVID-19 clinical trials

Vaccines and drugs are safe to use and crucial to protecting Canadians

The Government of Canada has strict regulations and testing requirements to ensure that authorized vaccines and drugs are safe to use. We continue to monitor and assess the safety and effectiveness of a product once it is on the Canadian market

  • How the Government of Canada ensures vaccines are safe for use
  • International statement about confidence in vaccines

Working with international partners to ensure vaccines are safe and widely adopted

Canada is working with international partners on a coordinated and well-aligned approach to this global pandemic. This ensures that health products are safe, effective and quickly available to Canadians. Collaboration also helps advance the development of diagnostics, treatments and vaccines that will save lives and protect the health and safety of people everywhere.

  • Canada has committed to funding global vaccinations for COVID-19
