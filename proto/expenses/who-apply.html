Eligibility criteria

To be eligible to claim expenses you paid for employment use of a work space in your home you must meet all of the following conditions:

  • Note: The requirement to work from home and pay for expenses related to a work space does not have to be part of your formal contract of employment. A verbal or written directive from your employer will be accepted.

    • Types of work spaces

      Note: If you work from home during COVID-19 for a continuous period of 4 weeks or more and mainly (more than 50% of the time) do your work from your work space in this period, you meet this condition. Refer to the COVID-19 examples

      or

    • or

    • What is T2200 (Short)

      Form T2200 (Short), Declaration of Conditions of Employment for Working at Home During COVID-19 is a shorter version of Form T2200 used specifically when employees were required to work from their home in 2020 due to COVID-19. It is completed by the employer to certify that an employee meets the conditions of employment and had to pay for home office expenses under their employment contract. The employer also identifies the period when an employee worked from home during COVID-19.

      or

    • What is T2200

      Form T2200, Declaration of Conditions of Employment is a form completed by the employer to certify that an employee meets the conditions of employment and had to pay for employment expenses under their employment contract.

    Note: Keep a copy for your records, in case we request it.

You need all of the above to be eligible to claim working from home expenses

COVID-19 examples

Working from home for 4 weeks or more due to COVID-19

Working situation

Kumi’s employer required her to work from home during April and May 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked exclusively from home and performed all of the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Kumi was required to work from home for a continuous period of 4 weeks or more in 2020 (that is, a continuous period of nine weeks) and she mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during that period, she may be able to claim home work space expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Working situation

Emma’s employer required her to work from home from April to October 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked an alternating schedule of three weeks at home and one week in the office. During the weeks Emma worked from home, she performed all of the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Emma was required to work from home for a continuous period of 4 weeks or more in 2020 (that is, a continuous period of 31 weeks) and she mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during that period, she may be able to claim home work space expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Working situation

Jason is a part-time employee who works three days a week at his employer’s office. From mid-March to July 2020, Jason’s employer required him to work from home due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During this period, he performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Jason was required to work from home for a continuous period of 4 weeks or more in 2020 (that is, a continuous period of 20 weeks) and he mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during that period, he may be able to claim home work space expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Working from home for less than 4 weeks due to COVID-19

Continuously working from home during that time

Working situation

Isaac’s employer required him to work from home for three weeks beginning in mid-March 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these three weeks, he worked exclusively from home and performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office. Following this three-week period, Isaac returned to his employer’s office for the rest of 2020 to perform all of his employment duties.

unsuccessful Result

Because Isaac was not required to work from home for a continuous period of 4 weeks or more in 2020 (that is, his employer required him to work from home for a continuous period of only three weeks), he cannot claim home work space expenses paid in that period.
